Annual Arts Exhibition Draws Hundreds of Visitors

Students from KG through JHS 3 displayed remarkable work at our biggest arts exhibition yet, attended by parents, alumni, and community members.

This is a placeholder article. Replace the content below with the real story when you are ready.

Annan House Community School opened its doors last Saturday for the annual Arts Exhibition, and the response from the community was overwhelming. More than 300 parents, alumni, and guests toured the school's corridors and hall, taking in an extraordinary display of paintings, sculptures, photography, and mixed-media work created by students at every level of the school.

A Showcase of Creativity

Exhibits ranged from vibrant KG drawings inspired by Ghanaian folktales to sophisticated JHS 3 photography projects exploring identity and urban life in Accra. The breadth of talent on display left many visitors speechless.

Community and Connection

One of the highlights of the day was a live mural-painting session in the school courtyard, where students worked collaboratively on a large piece celebrating the school's values of curiosity, community, and care.

Thank You

The school thanks everyone who attended and all the families who supported their children's creative journeys throughout the term. Your encouragement makes all the difference. Watch this space for dates for next year's exhibition.
