Second Term Ends on a High Note

Students and staff celebrated an outstanding second term with an end-of-term awards ceremony honouring academic and character achievements.

This is a placeholder article. Replace the content below with the real story when you are ready.

The second term of the 2025/2026 academic year came to a memorable close last week as Annan House Community School held its annual end-of-term awards ceremony in the school hall. Parents, guardians, and members of the school community gathered to celebrate the extraordinary effort and growth displayed by students across all year groups.

Celebrating Academic Excellence

Award categories ranged from Top Academic Performer and Most Improved Student to Subject Excellence and the Headteacher's Award for Character. Dozens of students were recognised across every level, from Creche through Cambridge Lower Secondary.

A Word from the Head Teacher

In her address, the head teacher praised students and staff for their resilience and focus throughout the term, noting that the results achieved reflect the dedication and partnership of families and the school community.

Looking Ahead

Third term begins on a date to be confirmed in the school calendar. Parents are reminded to check the notice board and school communication channels for updates on start dates, uniform requirements, and new activities for the term ahead.
